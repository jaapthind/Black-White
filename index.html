UN SDG 13: The Climate Emergency

Climate change is the defining crisis of our time. The United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 13 demands urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts. While energy and transportation get the most attention, material consumption and waste generation are massive drivers of global emissions.

Every product we consume required energy to extract raw materials, manufacture, and transport. When we throw items into a landfill, we destroy those embedded resources, forcing the cycle of extraction to begin again.

45%

Of global greenhouse gas emissions are tied to how we make, use, and dispose of products and food.

Immediate Impact

Changing how we manage waste is one of the fastest, most effective local actions a community can take.

The Methane Crisis

When organic matter (like food scraps, yard waste, and paper) is buried in a landfill, it is deprived of oxygen. It undergoes anaerobic decomposition.

This process releases Methane (CH₄), a super-pollutant greenhouse gas. Over a 20-year period, methane is roughly 80 times more potent at trapping heat in the atmosphere than carbon dioxide.

Landfill Emissions

Municipal solid waste landfills are the third-largest source of human-related methane emissions in the United States.

The Fix: Aerobic Compost

When organics go in the green bin, they decompose with oxygen, eliminating methane production entirely.

The Circular Economy

We must transition from a "Take-Make-Dispose" linear system to a Circular Economy. This means keeping materials in use for as long as possible and regenerating natural systems.

Proper sorting at the bin is the frontline of this transition. An aluminum can sorted correctly saves 95% of the energy needed to make a new one from raw bauxite ore. Clean organics sorted correctly become nutrient-rich compost that sequesters carbon back into agricultural soils.

Infinite Loops

Materials like aluminum and glass can be recycled infinitely without any loss of quality.

Carbon Drawdown

Applying compost to soil helps plants grow faster, pulling CO₂ out of the air and storing it in the ground.
